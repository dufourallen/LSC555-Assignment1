The Smithsonian Library System Introduction

Context

  • The Smithsonian Library (SIL) is a network of 21 specializes research libraries, providing the Smithsonian Institution’s museum and research centers with resources and services.
  • The Smithsonian Institution was founded by an Act of Congress in 1846. The first online public access catalog in 1985 and completing the project in 1999, although the digitization continues today.
  • The SIL digital books was built in Drupal, harvesting and storing metadata in Drupal while utilizing the Internet Archive to display content. The internal SIL collections are digitized in-house and have different image standards from resources available via the Internet Archive. “Vocabularies used in descriptions include Dublin Core, LCSH, AAT, SKOS, bibo, and Romaine subject headings for our Trade Literature.”

Users

  • The Smithsonian Library states that their target users are “visiting scholars, educators, students, and others pursuing or advancing knowledge in their fields”.

Content

  • The Smithsonian Library contains a vast amount of content that is organized within Online Books, Digital Collections, and Exhibitions. The site includes user tools and outreach within Research Tools, Education, and Get Involved.
  • The system provides information on each resource using either the Internet Archive’s Biblio software or the SIL’s internal MACAW (Metadata Collection and Workflow) tool: creator/s, subject/s, topic/s, which collection it is contained within, tag/s, copyright, citations, links to the DOI, additional access links, and download options.
  • “Total volumes owned by the Libraries exceed 2 million, more than 40,000 of which are rare books, complemented by 10,000 manuscripts. The Libraries houses the nation’s largest collection of commercial catalogs— 480,000, representing more than 35,000 companies dating from the 1810s. More than 180,000 microfilm and microfiche also are accessible for research in disciplines of science, history, art and technology.”

The Discussion

Interesting Features

  • The most interesting feature is the Index of Library and Archival Exhibitions on the Web. This index contains a collection of international and multi-lingual exhibitions created by libraries, archives, historical societies, and museums. It currently includes 7,161 exhibition links. There are simple and advanced search engines available. The simple search utilizes keywords and the advanced search utilizes broader browsing options like state/province, country, institutions, and a complete, alphabetized list.

Pros and Cons

  • SIL is a very thorough site.
  • There is a refreshing lack of advertising which makes it clean and usable.
  • The SIL home page is engaging and pulls the user downwards.
  • Some pages could use a visual update.
